This is a **sample academic piece** — replace it with your own scholarship.
The book reads any Markdown file dropped into a section folder and lays it out
in the Haus style, in chronological order.

> Time is the human thought failing to understand the unfolding universe at
> once.

A pull quote, set off from the body, renders as primary text — the way the
reference dossier treats its quotations. Use `>` at the start of a line to mark
one.

## A subsection

Academic prose is justified and set in Newsreader, the text serif. Headings
within a piece are set in Fraunces. Citations and labels are set in JetBrains
Mono. The three faces do the same work here that they do in the source
document.

Footnote-style references, dates, and source lines can sit in the body for now;
once writings are connected to the Lexicon, their provenance can be pulled in
automatically.
