▍The Doctrine

The Haus is held to one discipline: build what survives its encounter with the world. Its materials are matter and record, steel and glass and language, chosen the way a bench chooses them, for how they behave under load. Entropy is not treated as the enemy here; it is the condition, and design is the answer the Haus gives it in material form. Force is studied with respect and answered with structure: the panel that takes the second strike, the system that takes the second year, the sentence that takes the second reading. Impact is the only review that matters, and it files on its own schedule. What the Haus makes is meant to age into patina rather than failure, holding its coordinates while the surrounding weather moves. Creation is treated as a form of accounting: every made thing is a claim about what deserves to persist, entered in the register and kept. The ventures below are one architecture read in different materials. This page is the front door, and the door is load-bearing.

▍About

I build from the Rio Grande Valley's inheritance: a place that taught force as a first language, and a family that kept its tools close. I studied what breaks, at the bench and in the record, and turned the lesson toward what stops it: armor that meets the second strike, surfaces that meet the second decade, an archive built to meet the second century. The work runs between McAllen and Austin, most of it kept, all of it dated. The vow that governs it sits on the drafting table, not in the gun safe: I design nothing that fires, and I answer force with structure.
